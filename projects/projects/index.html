2015 – The Screen Space (Kickback Studios – Ludum Dare Entry)

A short game developed with Kickback Studios (creators of Lost Echo) as a Ludum Dare entry. I implemented the player’s controls, a jetpack mechanic and a rope-bucket mechanic that acted as a grappling hook. All of these mechanics were physics based.

Download: Click here (Web, Windows, OS/X, Linux). Development: Visual Studio, C#, Unity.line

2014 – 2D Audio Mixer

2daudiomixer

The dot in the circle is an audio listener, all the other dots are audio sources. The distance between the audio listener and an audio source is the volume. A pretty fun approach to audio mixing.

Download: Click here (Windows Store). Development: Visual Studio, C#, Unity (with NGUI).line

A turn-based game where you simply try to encircle a rat. The rat uses A* pathfinding.

2012 – Da Vinci’s Flying Machine

davincisflyingmachine

An infinite runner inspired by the works of Leonardo da Vinci. It won the 4th place and a best splash screen award at Microsoft hackathon that took place in Athens. I was forced to release in an alpha stage, however it did unexpectedly well and even made a few Windows Store payouts.

Download: No longer available. Development: Visual Studio, C#, MonoGame, Farseer Physics.

line2012 – Kinect Experimentations

line2011 – AV8

Collaborated with a talented student and guided by a rockstar professor on a project that enabled complete control over your PC (much like Minority Report) using Kinect.
